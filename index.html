Water Purity and Waste Data

Pure Water

Pure water sources, such as groundwater, are generally free from harmful contaminants but may contain natural minerals. In areas with minimal industrial impact, groundwater and mountain streams often represent the purest water sources. However, agricultural runoff can introduce nitrates, so regular testing is vital to ensure water quality.

Water Purity Levels

According to global data, nearly 90% of water sources in developed regions are safe, but in many developing areas, water is frequently contaminated by bacteria, heavy metals, and agricultural pollutants. The World Health Organization (WHO) recommends safe levels for these contaminants, yet studies show up to40% of water sources in high-risk regions do not meet these standards.

Waste-water

Wastewater generation has increased globally, with urban areas producing the highest amounts due to population density and industrial activity. While some developed nations treat over 70% of wastewater, many developing areas lack sufficient treatment infrastructure. This untreated wastewater can reintroduce contaminants into local ecosystems, impacting both water and food safety.
